Fixing timeout errors with Minecraft

When hosting a server from home, you may notice that some players get kicked due to timeout.
This happens when the Minecraft server is unable to provide data to the player fast enough.
There are two ways to fix/improve this

Solution #1


Are you on WiFi?

Use an Ethernet cable, most internet performance issues are due to bad WiFi. Unless you have the latest WiFi 6 router and are on 5Ghz with a line of sight, Ethernet is the best way to go when hosting a server at home.

If Ethernet is not an option, try getting closer to your WiFi Router / Access Point. Also, if you have other WiFi-enabled devices that you can turn off, give that a try it may also help.

Shut Programs Down

Turn off other programs that might be using your internet connection.
Depending on the technology used by your ISP to bring you internet, other users downloading or uploading data could dramatically affect your upload speed.
